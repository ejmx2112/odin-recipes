Tacos de Asada

Description

This recipe will help you cook up some juicy, steak tacos that'll make you feel like you're in an authentic restaurant. These tacos don't take too long to prepare and make for the perfect craving dish.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds flank steak

Marinade:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika

Onion Relish:

  • 1 white onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, juiced

Salsa:

  • 4 dried New Mexico chile pods
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 white onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 jalapeno peppers, chopped
  • 1 pinch salt and black pepper

Other:

  • 1 (32 ounce) package corn tortillas
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges

Directions

  1. Lay out steak in a large glass dish.
  2. Make the marinade by whisking all corresponding ingredients together. Pour marinade over steak and flip to coat both sides. Cover dish and marinate in the fridge for 1-8 hour(s).
  3. Make the salsa by preheating oven to 450 degrees F and heating skillet over medium-high heat. Add chiles and toast for few minutes; transfer pods to a bowl of water for 30 minutes.
  4. Place tomatoes, 1 onion, 4 cloves of garlic, and jalapenos onto a baking sheet. Roast in oven until toasted, about 20 minutes.
  5. Put vegetables into blender. Add soaked chiles, salt, and pepper; puree until smooth.
  6. Remove steak from marinade and shake off excess. Cut steak into strips. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add steak; cook and stiur until well-cooked.
  7. Warm tortillas in skillet over low heat.
  8. Place steak on each tortilla, top with relish and salsa. Garnish with lime wedges and serve.
